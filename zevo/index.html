We believe we can help Zevo turn more vehicle views into completed first-trip bookings.

What we see

Peer-to-peer EV sharing depends on the renter feeling clear about the vehicle, the host, charging, and handoff. The first trip flow is where that confidence has to come together.

Opportunities to explore

Three places we'd start in the product.

01

Put host, vehicle, and charging clarity on the listing

We'd start by combining host signals, vehicle condition, range, and charging expectations before the renter opens checkout.

Vehicle view → Trip clarity → Booking started
02

Design pickup and return as one guided handoff

Worth testing a renter checklist that covers access, charging level, photos, and return instructions in the same pattern.

03

Adapt EV-specific guidance by vehicle model

We'd look at lightweight model tips for first-time renters, especially charging, range, and app access differences.

Ways to measure success

A few ideas for leading indicators we could track with you.

  • ·Vehicle views that become booking starts and confirmed first trips.
  • ·Pickup checklist completion among new renters.
  • ·Repeat booking or saved-vehicle behavior after the first trip.
