We believe we can help Book An Artist move more project briefs to paid commission requests.

What we see

Commission buyers need to describe a creative outcome before they know which artist will make it. The brief flow has to turn uncertainty about style, budget, timing, and location into a request artists can answer.

Opportunities to explore

Three places we'd start in the product.

01

Guide the brief around the decision artists need

We'd start by structuring the request around surface, style, location, deadline, and budget so buyers feel guided without writing from scratch.

Project idea → Guided brief → Artist matched
02

Show comparable commissions before payment

Worth testing examples that match the buyer's category and budget before they send a deposit or commit to outreach.

03

Clarify artist response status

We'd look at a project timeline that tells buyers when an artist has reviewed, quoted, asked a question, or accepted.

Ways to measure success

A few ideas for leading indicators we could track with you.

  • ·Project brief starts that become paid commission requests.
  • ·Artist response and quote rates by category, region, and budget range.
  • ·Buyer reactivation after quote, revision, or artist decline events.
